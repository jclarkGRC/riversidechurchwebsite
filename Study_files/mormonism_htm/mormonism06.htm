5. Contradictions and errors:

a. The Book of Mormon contradicts the Bible and history by stating that the Messiah would be born at Jerusalem (Alma 7:10). Some Mormons claim that the reference to Jerusalem refers to the general area so there is no mistake. Besides the fact that the general area was usually referred to as Judea, 1 Nephi 1:4 describes Jerusalem as a city, so there is no escaping the error.

b. It also contradicts Doctrine and Covenants about a controversial issue. In Section 132 of D & C, Smith justifies polygamy on the basis that "God commanded Abraham" and "Sarah gave Hagar to Abraham to wife", therefore we should "do the works of Abraham." He also refers to David’s "many wives and concubines." But in Jacob 2:24, 26-27 of the Book of Mormon we see a different story. Monogamy is required.

QUESTION: Which was written later and why do you suppose it was written that way? As we have seen, Doctrines and Covenants was written later which implies that Smith decided that he wanted more than one woman to wife and tried to justify it through a new "revelation."

c. There is also a contradiction about the importance and nature of baptism. In the Book of Mormon (3 Nephi 12:2; Moroni 8:11) the remission of sins is the result of water baptism. But in Doctrines and Covenants section 20:37 those who show by "works" that "they have received ... the spirit of Christ unto the remission of their sins" shall be "received by baptism into his church."

NOTE: This particular revelation almost caused a riot among the Saints. Whereas before they only had to be baptized to have their sins forgiven, now there was a much more stringent requirement.

Conclusion: These then are the works upon which Mormons base their lives and their hopes of life after death. We have only skimmed the surface of the multitudes of inaccuracies, contradictions and revisions. Surely, we can conclude that these works are a prime example of the deceptions and lies that Satan places before the unsaved to keep them in the dark away from the true Jesus of the Bible.
