II. Beliefs

A. About God

Mormons try to claim that they believe in and worship the God of the Bible, the same God that true Christians worship. To this end, their Articles of Faith #1 says "We believe in God, the Eternal Father, and His Son Jesus Christ, and in the Holy Ghost." Richard L. Evans, a member of the Council of Twelve, said Mormons "believe in the Holy Trinity." Both of these statements are demonstrably false. Below is a chart comparing/contrasting historical Christian positions concerning God and what Mormons believe:

Characteristics of the Christian God Characteristics of the Mormon God
1. One (monotheism) 1. Many (polytheism or henotheism)
2. Immutable (unchanging) 2. Evolving (changing)
3. Immaterial (spirit) 3. Material (physical)
4. Nonsexual 4. Sexual
5. Celibate 5. Polygamist
6. Eternally holy 6. Morally imperfect (requires salvation)

** Supporting quotations and evidence of what Mormons really believe:

1. Polytheism (Henotheism is a polytheistic system stressing one central deity, i.e., "Elohim" is the primary god of this earth so Mormons worship him but acknowledge there are other gods in existence for other worlds.)

a. Joseph Smith- "I wish to declare I have always and in all congregations where I have preached on the subject of deity , it has (been on) the plurality of Gods." (J.F. Smith, Teachings of the Prophet, p. 370.)

b. Bruce McConkie- "There are three Gods- the Father, Son and Holy Ghost." And: " ... these three are the only Gods we worship. But, in addition, there is an infinite number of holy personages, drawn from worlds without number, who have passed on to exaltation and are thus Gods ..." (Mormon Doctrine, pp. 317, 576-77.)

c. Abraham 4:1 - "And then the Lord said: Let us go down. And they went down at the beginning, and they, that is the Gods, organized and formed the heavens and the earth."

-- Compare to Isaiah 43:10; 44:6,8; 45:5, 21-22 from which Christians affirm there is only one true, eternal God. Mormons are quick to point out the Bible's references to other individuals as "gods." See II Corinthians 4:4; Exodus 4:16, 7:1; Psalm 82:1,6. In each case the context gives us the meaning of the usage. In no case is the Bible comparing the one being called a "god" to the eternal, almighty God of the universe. A good verse to respond to this implication is in I Corinthians 8:4-6 where Paul says the world may have many lords and gods, but there is only one true God. Polytheism is totally foreign to traditional Christian theology. According to Alma 11:26-29 it would seem that polytheism is also foreign to the Book of Mormon. (Another contradiction?)

2. An evolving god.

a. Joseph Smith- "God Himself was once as we are now, and is an exalted man and sits enthroned in yonder heavens! ... We have imagined and supposed that God was God from all eternity. I will refute that idea ... He was once a man like us. Here, then, is eternal life -- to know the only wise and true God; and you have got to learn how to be Gods yourselves." (J.F. Smith, Teachings of the Prophet, pp. 345-46 [quoting Joseph Smith’s King Follett Discourse].)

b. Brigham Young-"Remember that God, our Heavenly Father, was perhaps once a child, and mortal like we ourselves, and rose step by step in the scale of progress, in the school of advancement; has moved forward and overcome until He has arrived at the point where He now is." (Journal of Discourses, Vol. 1, p. 123.)

c. Milton Hunter- "Mormon prophets have continually taught the sublime truth that God the Eternal Father was once a mortal man who passed through a school of earth life similar to that through which we are now passing. He became God -- an exalted being -- through obedience to the same eternal Gospel truths that we are given opportunity today to obey." (The Gospel Through the Ages, p. 104.)

-- Compare to Numbers 23:19a; Psalm 90:2; Hosea 11:9; Malachi 3:6a; James 1:17. Again the traditional, biblical Christian view is that God has always been God. He was never a created being (a man) and He never changes. This is in stark contrast with the Mormon opinion.

3. A material god.

a. Joseph Smith-"There is no other God in heaven but that God that has flesh and bones." (Joseph F. Smith, Teachings of the Prophet, p. 181.) And- " ... if you were to see him today, you would see him in all the person, image and very form as a man.”" (Sermon published Aug. 15, 1844.)

b. Parley P. Pratt- "Each of these Gods, including Jesus Christ and His Father, being in possession of not merely an organized spirit, but a glorious immortal body of flesh and bones ... "(Key to the Science of Theology, p. 44.)

c. Bruce McConkie- "God, the Eternal Father, our Father in Heaven, is an exalted, perfected, and glorified Personage having a tangible body of flesh and bones." (Mormon Doctrine, p. 278.)

d. Doctrine and Covenants 130:22- "The Father has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's;"

-- Compare Luke 24:39; John 4:24. God the Father is portrayed in the Bible as a spirit being. Nowhere is there any indication that He has a physical body. When Scriptures speak of the eyes, ears, arms, hands, etc. of the Lord, they are merely literary devices to help humans relate to Him in a way that makes sense. They refer to His attributes of seeing, hearing (being aware of all things), His power and strength and so on. Again, we see the Mormon idea of God having a real, flesh and blood, albeit "exalted" (improved) body is not what the Bible teaches or what Christianity has historically embraced.

4. A sexual god. (This is a natural outgrowth of the Mormon belief that God has a physical body.)

a. Bruce McConkie- "We are the offspring of God. He is our eternal father; we also have an eternal mother. There is no such thing as a father without a mother, nor can there be children without parents. We were born as the spirit children of celestial parents long before the foundations of this world were laid." (Doctrinal New Testament Commentary, Vol. 2, p. 160.)

-- The Mormon teaching is essentially that after attaining exaltation (or godhood), a man and his celestial wife (or wives) then sexually beget spirit children. Closely tied to this is the Mormon view that God is a polygamist.

5. God is a polygamist.

a. Brigham Young- "The scripture says He, the Lord, came walking in the Temple with His train; I do not know who they were, unless his wives and children." (Journal of Discourses, Vol. 13, p. 308).

b. Apostle Orson Pratt- "In the Heaven where our spirits were born, there are many Gods, each one of whom has his own wife or wives which were given to him previous to his redemption, while yet in mortal state." Pratt further commented that God honors polygamy because He is Himself a polygamist. Therefore His Son (Jesus) and His people are also polygamists. (The Seer, Vol. 1, No. 11, p. 172.)

FYI: Thus there are two reasons Mormons rationalize and justify polygamy (or at least have in the past). One is that they are only following the example set by God. The second is that all those spirit children in heaven are waiting on a physical body to take their turn at life on earth. If one wife can produce 10 children (physical bodies), then 20 wives can produce 200. Hence, the more wives the better.

-- There is absolutely no biblical indication or even a hint that God is married, sexually active or that He reproduces Himself in any way. God is unique and He never changes. There are only two teachings about marriage in heaven. The first is when Jesus informed the Sadducees that those who are resurrected and go to heaven neither marry nor are given in marriage ... period! Cf. Matthew 22:29-30; Mark 12:24-25; Luke 20:34-36. The other concerns the "marriage of the Lamb" (cf. Revelation 19:7, 9) which, though somewhat ambiguous, seems to refer to the union of Christ and all those who believe in Him for salvation. It is metaphorical for a union which will never be broken and is characterized by the deepest love possible on the part of both parties. Again, the Bible has nothing within it to even suggest that God is sexual and has a wife or many wives. The idea is ludicrous.

6. A morally imperfect god.

a. Milton Hunter- "Mormon prophets have continually taught the sublime truth that God the Eternal Father was once a mortal man who passed through a school of earth life similar to that through which we are now passing. He became God -- an exalted being -- through obedience to the same eternal Gospel truths that we are given opportunity today to obey." (The Gospel Through the Ages, p. 104.)

b. Brigham Young- ""How much unbelief exists in the minds of the Latter-day Saints in regard to one particular doctrine which I revealed to them, and which God revealed to me -- namely that Adam is our father and God ... 'Well,' says one, 'Why was Adam called Adam?' He was the first man on earth and its framer and maker. He with the help of his brethren, brought it into existence. Then he said, 'I want my children who are in the spirit world to come and live here. I once dwelt upon an earth something like this, in a mortal state. I was faithful, I received my crown and exaltation.'" (Deseret Weekly News, June 18, 1873, p.308)

c. Marion G. Romney- "God is a perfected saved soul enjoying eternal life." (Gospel Doctrine, quoted in the Salt Lake Tribune, Oct. 6, 1974.)

NOTE: In essence, all the gods of the Mormon religion were imperfect men who had to earn exaltation through obedience to Mormon articles of faith. The obvious questions are: Where did the Mormon gospel originate? Who was the very first god? Who discovered these truths?

-- Compare Leviticus 19:2 and Revelation 4:8 which tell us that God is holy. We have already looked at Scripture that shows us He is unchanging. God is eternally holy and has never been in need of salvation or justification or of becoming morally perfect. He has always had those qualities.

In an attempt to bolster their own doctrines, Mormons claim that orthodox Christianity is in a state of apostasy and has lost the true knowledge of God. After having examined LDS beliefs about God, it would appear that Mormons have more in common with pagans, who create their own gods with human characteristics, attributes and imperfections, than they do with authentic, historic Christianity. One wonders why they consistently identify themselves as a Christian religion when they are in complete disagreement with biblical Christian teachings about God that has been proclaimed and accepted since the earliest days of the Church.
