Question 2: Will the current dwelling place of God, aka Heaven in the Bible, be the place where Christians will spend eternity?

This may seem an odd question. But I think it's important to get a proper understanding of heaven to start our journey. When we start to consider concepts like "death" or "the afterlife" or "eternal destiny" there's a feeling of finality. Rightly so, because each person's ultimate destination is determined at death. The Bible does not teach "purgatory" (Catholicism) or "spirit prison" (Mormonism).

However, that sense of finality has caused some to perhaps be mistaken about our ultimate destination. Because we know that true believers go to "Heaven" and we know that "Heaven" is where the Father currently resides and that Jesus is at His right hand (Acts 7:56) and that it is our immediate destination when we die (Philip. 1:21-23) and we will always be with the Lord (I Thess. 4:13-17), therefore (some believe) the current place called "Heaven" must be our final and eternal destination and home.

But is that necessarily true? If not, what is another possibility? What if the Lord Himself moves on from the current "Heaven"? Impossible? Wait! Not so fast! Take a look at Revelation 21:1-3! Hear what Randy Alcorn writes about this passage:

Heaven, God's dwelling place, will one day be on the New Earth. Notice that the New Jerusalem, which was in Heaven, will come down out of Heaven from God. Where does it go? To the New Earth. From that time on, "the dwelling of God" will be with redeemed mankind on Earth. Some would argue that the New Earth shouldn't be called Heaven. But it seems clear to me that if God's special dwelling place is by definition Heaven, and we're told that "the dwelling of God" will be with mankind on Earth, then Heaven and the New Earth will be essentially the same place. ... Again, it seems clear that wherever God dwells with his people and sits on his throne (Revelation 22:1) would be called Heaven. ... That God would come down to the New Earth to live with us fits perfectly with his original plan. God could have taken Adam and Eve up to Heaven to visit with him in his world. Instead, he came down to walk with them in their world (Genesis 3:8). Jesus says of anyone who would be his disciple, "My Father will love him, and we will come to him and make our home with him" (John 14:23). This is a picture of God's ultimate plan -- not to take us up to live in a realm made for him, but to come down and live with us in the realm he made for us. (Heaven, p.45, emphases his.)

Does this idea seem disappointing? On the contrary it should excite us! Instead of looking ahead to being stuck on a cloud with only a harp for eternity (as some incorrectly paint the dreary picture) we can anticipate finally getting to explore this great, big and much improved world with no limits on our time, resources, abilities and friends (more on that later).

So is it incorrect to say, "I'm a Christian bound for Heaven!"? No, it's absolutely correct! What is incorrect is the notion that the place we currently call Heaven, where God's throne is now, will be our home for eternity. Think of it like an airline flight. You have a final destination and when asked, you respond with that last stop. It may be that you have a change of planes in another city and that may come up as you talk about the trip, but the layover is not the final destination upon which you are focused. Just so, if we die before the Lord returns, we will have a layover in the place we will call the intermediate Heaven. It's not permanent but neither is it unimportant because people we know are there now. So we will next begin considering what we can about the intermediate Heaven. After that, we will turn our attention to the final destination.

We have seen that the Bible teaches that the final "Heaven" where we will spend eternity is actually the New Earth upon which the New Jerusalem will descend and where Jesus will reign forever. The current "Heaven" where God the Father and Jesus are now dwelling is only a temporary place waiting until the Lord renews all things. For the purposes of this study we are calling it the "Intermediate Heaven". We will examine what the Bible teaches about this place since Christian people we know and love are there (and we ourselves as believers will also go there if Jesus tarries!).
