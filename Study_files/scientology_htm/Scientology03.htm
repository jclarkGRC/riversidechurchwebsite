Bait and switch tactics

As the pamphlet related, the information we now know is not shared with potential recruits at first (much as Mormons do not start out by telling people that one day they can possibly each be a god with their own world to rule). Most people would recognize the fantastic nature of such a crazy story. In fact the author of the pamphlet, Rashleigh-Berry, was an avid Scientologist until he reached the level OT III mentioned in the text and heard the story about Xenu. D isgusted, he immediately quit and made it one of his goals to reveal the sham for what it was, hence, his writing and publication of the Xenu leaflet (www.xenu.net/archive/leaflet/#comments).

But what attracted him in the first place and what attracts many others to read Dianetics and to consider getting involved with the Scientology religion and its processes? The answer is that Scientology uses a carrot -- a powerful and intriguing carrot. Scientology tells people that they can be happy (e.g., Scientology: A New Slant on Life chapter one is entitled 'Is It Possible to Be Happy?' ). Who wouldn't want to be happy?

Furthermore, this happiness is entirely within the capabilities of any person to attain for himself. The idea of self-sufficiency and self-improvement appeals to many. In the just referenced Scientology book, Hubbard is quoted just before the table of contents:

The human mind is capable of resolving the problem of the human mind.
And from the introductory guide What is Scientology? we are informed that:
Although modern life seems to pose an infinitely complex array of problems, Scientology maintains that the solutions to those problems are basically simple and within every man's reach. Difficulties with communication and interpersonal relationships, nagging insecurities, self-doubt and despair -- each man innately possesses the potential to be free of these and many other concerns. (p. 59)

There is also a constant and confident insistence on their part that Scientology is new, that it really works and that it is the only thing that does really work. In a 1967 journal entry Hubbard writes:

In all the broad universe there is no other hope for Man than ourselves.
These kinds of assertions can be persuasive when from a true believer. For instance, look up Tom Cruise's testimonial interview on YouTube sometime when you feel like having a good laugh (or maybe a good fright).

Man's basic drive

As mentioned earlier, Hubbard did not start out by telling people about Xenu, the tyrannical galactic overlord. In point of fact he could not have done so since the best evidence suggests this information was not 'discovered' by him until 1967. On the other hand the book which launched this entire movement, Dianetics: The Modern Science of Mental Health, was completed in 1950.

That book (and subsequent works) makes several 'bold' assertions while at the same time admitting that the basic ideas are not new. It is the nuance or the twist that Hubbard gives to each idea which makes it unique, new and groundbreaking. For instance, in the book Hubbard reveals that man is a spiritual being. While admitting that other philosophies and religions have likewise asserted this, he contrasts Dianetics (and eventually Scientology) with modern science which declares man's mind to be just a "a collection of cells and neurons" (What is Scientology?, p. 60) and which says that man himself is just flesh or "mud" (Personal interview with Hubbard in 1966 published on video as An Introduction to Scientology). He then calls each spirit a thetan which wears and uses a mind and body. Thus the individual is ultimately and essentially the thetan while the mind and body are tools for his/her/it's use.

What it uses them for is survival (another marvelous insight on his part). Once again it is conceded that this is not a brand new idea but what differentiates Hubbard's contention is that he says that all of man's activities can be linked or related to this one primary, supreme and overriding goal to "SURVIVE!" (What is Scientology?, p. 60.) This drive to survive is not limited to just staying alive but is concerned with doing so on as high a level as possible. Thus Hubbard lays out what eventually became known as the Eight Dynamics which enumerate and describe what mankind's o ptimal survival looks like. The first four were presented in Dianetics while the rest were developed after Hubbard turned the dianetic process into the religion of Scientology. They are as follows:

  1. The dynamic of SELF -- your own individual survival.
  2. The dynamic of CREATIVITY -- has to do with leaving a legacy through family and children.
  3. The dynamic of GROUP SURVIVAL -- being part of a larger community (school, work, etc.).
  4. The dynamic of SPECIES -- being part of the human race.
  5. The dynamic of LIFE FORMS -- all life on earth not just humanity.
  6. The dynamic of the PHYSICAL UNIVERSE -- the desire of the universe to survive.
  7. The dynamic of SPIRITUAL -- the survival of 'being,' i.e., life force itself.
  8. The dynamic of INFINITY -- the urge to having an eternal, infinite existence. (Ibid., pp. 70-71.)
The goal is to be proficiently surviving in all dynamics simultaneously.

Man's basic problem

However, according to Hubbard, most men and women have a hang-up which hinders them from these optimal levels of survival. The problem is rooted in the fact that mankind, in general, does not know himself. Man does not understand that the adverse aspects of life are caused in large part by what is happening in and through the mind. Such things as negative "emotions, irrational fears and psychosomatic illnesses" are "most accurately described as what the soul is doing to the body through the mind". (Ibid., p. 60.) Thus the word 'dianetics' was coined from Greek 'dia' meaning 'through' and 'nous' meaning 'mind'.

According to Hubbard the mind records every detail of every moment of life using mental image pictures and puts it on a 'time track' which is likened to a motion picture film. His 'breakthrough' was that he 'discovered' that the mind consists of two parts: analytical and reactive. The analytical mind is that part which observes, records consciously, reasons, and resolves problems. It loads information into 'standard memory banks ... and uses the data to make decisions that promote survival' (or, in other words, a better life). (Ibid., p. 63)

There is one major exception to the recordings made by the analytical mind according to Hubbard's theory, which is, that in moments of extreme duress the reactive mind supersedes the analytical mind. The reactive mind (or subconscious) is cruder and less logical than the analytical mind but it is very sturdy and up to the task of handling the painful moments of life. It still records in perfect detail everything that is happening but stores the information in separate memory banks which cannot be readily accessed by the analytical (or conscious) mind. These painful, unpleasant mental images are called engrams. It is the presence of engrams in man which hinders him from attaining his highest and best survival status. In short, engrams are the source of all our problems. How so? Read on.

The evil which 'engrams' engender

Because the recordings made by the mind are so precise, when a traumatic event occurs all the surrounding details are included. So in the engram all sounds, smells, tastes, faces, activity, etc. are remembered. But because the reactive mind is so much less sophisticated than the analytical mind it equates these other, mostly neutral details as being part of the negative episode. For instance, a person is out driving and has a bad automobile accident which injures him. In the background is the sound of a jackhammer, in the air is the smell of a nearby fast food restaurant, up close are the excited voices of some Hispanics rushing to help, speaking Spanish, and blinking over the whole scene is a bright flashing neon light. At the moment of the accident, the reactive mind takes over and records all these things and more and buries it all deep in the subconscious.

The idea of Dianetics is that at a later point in time that same person may hear a jackhammer or hear someone speaking Spanish excitedly or smell the same fast food or see a bright flashing light and experience extreme nervousness or feel out of control or even feel the pain of the now healed injury. This reawakening of the old engram is called restimulation. The reactive mind, being so crude and illogical, has connected all those other irrelevant factors to the accident and injury and in the moment of hearing, smelling or seeing similar things immediately takes over and tries to steer the person away from what it views as a another threatening situation by bringing up the old bad feelings. These negative feelings or emotions are referred to as commands. The person has no choice in the matter of what he is currently experiencing as a result of these activating, embedded memories.

The reactive mind is, in its own way, also trying to facilitate survival but it is now a hindrance rather than a help. It causes the person to act irrationally or, as Dianetics puts it, he is in a state of aberration (wandering from a straight path or line). The person is not aware of how he is being affected in this way, it is all done on a subconscious level. In fact his analytical mind actually begins to try to supply an explanation for the sudden onset of these feelings, anxieties, or even physical pain. This is called 'justified thought' by Hubbard.

NOTE: In the 2010 DVD How to Use Dianetics it is asserted that in the lower forms of life this function of the reactive mind is still a valuable trait but in humans it has outlasted its usefulness. We've come a long way, baby!

The point, according to Hubbard, is that all our issues in all areas of life come directly or indirectly from the presence of engrams. We do not know when they were created (some were perhaps formed while we were still in the womb). We do not have any idea how detrimental they are. We do not even realize they are there. And we have no conception of how good life can be without them.

NOTE: One comparison used by Hubbard is that someone who has no engrams can be compared to a normal person to the same degree that the normal person can be compared to someone who is clinically insane.

When a person reaches this state (referred to as 'clear') amazing things begin to happen. For the first time he is able to operate at the levels of which he is capable. This is no mere small step of improvement in a single characteristic, natural ability, or acquired skill but, rather, it is a profound enormous leap of dramatic change on all levels of existence. How to get 'Clear'

But how is it done? How can a person get rid of those pesky engrams which are the source of all the problems in life? Simple ... go through dianetic processing, also called auditing. What is that, you say?

It is essentially when a person who believes he needs help to get rid of his reactive mind experiences (who are called preclears) allows himself to be led through those negative memories. After completely revealing them, the theory is that he becomes more aware of his own identity and true nature and then, with the help of the auditor who provides extra dynamic thrust (mental/emotional/spiritual force?), these engrams are canceled. What does that look and sound like? The auditor asks questions or gives commands -- sometimes to get him to come to a realization which becomes more or less automatic; or perhaps to lead the preclear to recount in detail those bad experiences. Perhaps the best way to demonstrate is to read from two Scientology publications, guidebooks which accompany DVD presentations -- Scientology: The Fundamentals of Thought (pp. 15-16) and How to Use Dianetics (pp. 13-26).

New developments

As we have seen, Hubbard made some pretty bold claims for what Dianetic auditing will do for a person, in essence it will make his life perfect. However, that did not (and does not) happen. Despite being declared Clear, people still had bad memories or lost their temper or had down days and so on. This was a problem. The fact that auditing ultimately does not work as it purports to do contributed to the need for further 'revelation.'

This led to the addition of the spiritual aspect which eventually developed into Scientology. The 1951 publication Science of Survival introduced the idea of the theta (or spirit) having an existence separate from the physical universe of matter, energy, space and time (referred to as MEST). The theta is senior to and the source of MEST. Each individual is referred to as a thetan (or thought unit). The thetan is the real person which is immortal and can exist independently from the mind and body which it uses to interact and relate to the physical universe.

When a person's body dies and the mind ceases to function then the thetan moves on -- usually to another body. Thus Hubbard incorporated the eastern concept of reincarnation into his evolving 'theology.'

Do I detect a certain 'Tone?'

The degree of compatibility of the theta and MEST as they relate to each other is very important. The better they interact, the better an individual 'survives.' This compatibility is measured by Scientologists on what they call the Tone scale. The range goes from 0.0 (which is 'Body Death') up to 40.0 ('Serenity of Beingness'). In between are many categories of a person's 'being' set off with a number and a one or two word description. These descriptive words are typically emotions (cf. What is Scientology?, pp. 72-73) which Hubbard claims reflect either the ease or the difficulty of the interplay between theta and MEST in a given person's life.

The critical level, according to Hubbard seems to be 2.0 which is labeled 'Antagonism.' Anyone at that level or below seems to fit into the category of having a life which is not worthy to be lived. Consider some of the following quotations from http://www.xenu.net/archive/infopack/5.htm:

In any event, any person from 2.0 down on the Tone Scale should not have, in any thinking society, any civil rights of any kind, because by abusing those rights he brings into being arduous and strenuous laws which are oppressive to those who need no such restraints. - L. Ron Hubbard, Science of Survival, 1989 Ed., p. 145 [The "Tone Scale" is Scientology's measure of mental and spiritual health.]
There are only two answers for the handling of people from 2.0 down on the Tone Scale, neither one of which has anything to do with reasoning with them or listening to their justification of their acts. The first is to raise them on the Tone Scale by un-enturbulating some of their theta by any one of the three valid processes. The other is to dispose of them quietly and without sorrow. - L. Ron Hubbard, Science of Survival, p. 170.
The sudden and abrupt deletion of all individuals occupying the lower bands of the Tone Scale from the social order would result in an almost instant rise in the cultural tone and would interrupt the dwindling spiral into which any society may have entered. - L. Ron Hubbard, Science of Survival, p. 170.
A Venezuelan dictator once decided to stop leprosy. He saw that most lepers in his country were also beggars. By the simple expedient of collecting and destroying all the beggars in Venezuela an end was put to leprosy in that country. - L. Ron Hubbard, Science of Survival, p. 171.

Whether that last reference to an alleged genocide in Venezuela actually happened is unknown (given his habit of making things up, it seems doubtful), nevertheless the fact that Hubbard approves it as a positive thing reveals his estimation of human life. Once again, Hubbard's impatience and disdain for those he evaluates as not up to par becomes obvious.

Going from 'Clear' to 'OT'

The goal, of course, is to raise your rating on the Tone Scale so that your life improves (and so you don't become a target for elimination). However, as we have seen, the auditing didn't produce to the extent promised. What could be the problem? Return now to the idea of reincarnation. The basic levels of auditing only dealt with the experiences you've had in this life. But since you've had many lives and each one had negative experiences, those engrams are still affecting your 'thetannessicity' (OK, I admit it -- I made that term up myself. See how easy it is?). All the negative incidents on a person's time track must be addressed. Once they are, then the individual can expect that he or she will begin to experience some of the amazing changes and benefits which auditing is alleged to effect.

Progressing in this way on 'The Bridge to Total Freedom' (their term this time) is the key to restoring a person to his native state, as it were. In other words he or she is working toward becoming an 'operating thetan' or O.T. for short. Recalling that a thetan has control over MEST, the ultimate promise is that a person who moves toward and reaches the final destination of O.T. 8 then he or she will be able to create and control his or her reality. These god-like abilities are another carrot Scientology holds out to further entice recruits and keep their current crop hooked.

(One woman said that after she was introduced to this concept, she had to consciously make herself not rub her arms and body in an attempt to wipe away the multitudes of invisible beings clinging to her.) Drug problems in prior lives are also addressed at this level.

At O.T. 5-7 the very important lesson of how to telepathically ask one's body thetans, at least those which are conscious enough to respond, to desist and depart is learned. The person who completes level 7 is said to be an 'operating thetan.'

A convenient religious awakening

Of course, all this activity did not go unnoticed. Soon governmental authorities and organizations were beginning to nose around seeing what was going on with Hubbard and his Dianetics.

Almost from the beginning, Hubbard had been in trouble with the law. In 1951 the New Jersey Board of Medical Examiners brought proceedings against him for teaching medicine without a license; he fled to Los Angeles to escape prosecution. His organizations were repeatedly charged with practicing medicine without a license; E-meters and vitamin compounds were seized. The FDA accused Scientology of falsely claiming the E-meter could cure medical ailments and all E-meters were required to carry labels disavowing such claims. (From A Brief History of the Church of Scientology found at www.xenu.net/archive/infopack/8.htm)

It was shortly after this that the Church of Scientology came into existence:

In 1953 Hubbard regained control of Dianetics after a protracted legal battle and incorporated the Church of Scientology, Church of American Science and Church of Spiritual Engineering. In 1954 he incorporated the Church of Scientology of California, which became the mother church. In 1956 the church was granted US federal tax-exempt status. (Ibid.)

In that same 1983 interview, L. Ron Hubbard offers insight on why Hubbard went this route:

  • Magazine: And what is the Church of Scientology?
  • Hubbard: It's one of my father's many organizations. It was formed in 1953, basically to avoid the harassment of my father by the medical profession and the IRS. The idea of Scientology didn't really exist before that point as a religion, but my father hit upon turning it into a church after he started feeling pressured.
